Recommended Direction

Direction B (Split Identity) — chrome for BRAN, white for SOL — creates a dual tension: the iridescent creative side balanced by the grounded, structural side. Most distinctive at scale.

Minimum Size

Never render the wordmark below 120px wide in digital or 30mm in print. Chrome gradient loses fidelity below this threshold.

Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the wordmark on all four sides. No competing visual elements within this zone.

06 · Brand Rules

What BRANSOL Does & Doesn't Do

DO

Use the rainbowchrome gradient only on primary actions, the wordmark, and key accent moments. Restraint is what makes it powerful.
Lead with dark backgrounds. The chrome reads at its most powerful against deep void tones — #060608 or #0D0D12.
Use glassmorphism for cards, overlays, and panels. Blur values between 20–32px. Always with a chrome or white border at low opacity.
Write in short, declarative sentences. No sentence needs to be longer than it earns the right to be.
Let negative space breathe. Premium brands are not afraid of emptiness — it signals confidence.
Use Syne for all display and heading weights. Use Instrument Serif italic for editorial moments. DM Mono for everything functional.

DON'T

Apply the chrome gradient to body text, backgrounds, or decorative fills. It loses all authority when used everywhere.
Use light backgrounds as a primary environment. BRANSOL lives in dark. Light is reserved for reversed lockups only.
Stack glass effects on glass effects. One layer of glassmorphism per visual plane. More than one creates visual mud.
Use exclamation marks, question hooks, or engagement-bait language. BRANSOL states — it doesn't ask for permission.
Use purple gradients on white, Inter/Roboto/Arial, or any pattern that reads as generic SaaS or generic "AI aesthetic."
Show pricing, tiers, or subscription language anywhere in the brand. Investment is a private conversation.
