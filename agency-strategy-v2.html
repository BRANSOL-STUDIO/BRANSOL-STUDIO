Your White Space

Three Differentiators None of Your Competitors Can Claim

◈

A Proprietary Client Platform

DesignJoy uses Trello. Design Pickle has a workflow platform built for throughput. Superside has tech buried in their enterprise product. Yours is a bespoke relationship environment — built around the client, not the queue.

None of them built a platform as a client relationship tool. Yours is unique.
◉

Outcomes Over Hours

Superside, DesignJoy, and Design Pickle all sell time in different packages. You sell results. Your clients aren't buying hours of creative labour — they're commissioning transformation. That framing puts you in a completely different market.

All three competitors are trapped in the time-for-money model. You aren't.
◎

Formal Commission Capability

None of these three can credibly respond to a structured brief, present formal credentials, or operate within a procurement process. That entire market — organisations that require selection, vetting, and accountability — is yours alone to serve.

The formal commissioning market is structurally inaccessible to all three competitors.

04 · Your Proprietary Advantage

The Client Platform — How to Position & Show It

Not a tool. An environment.

Your platform is the most tangible proof of your premium positioning. It's not a feature — it's evidence that you've invested in your client relationships at an infrastructure level. Position it that way.

📋

Centralised Brief Management

Every request, revision, and approval lives in one place. Clients always know where things stand. No chasing emails, no lost files, no version confusion. Structure signals professionalism from day one.

🔍

Real-Time Project Visibility

Clients can see progress against milestones without asking. Status updates are automatic. This removes the most common anxiety in client-agency relationships: "where is my project?"

💬

Structured Feedback Loops

Feedback is given in context, pinned to deliverables, and tracked to resolution. No interpretation gaps. Every revision is documented — protecting both client and studio.

📁

Secure Asset Delivery

Files are delivered, versioned, and archived through the platform. Clients always have access to every asset, at any time, without needing to ask. Final asset packs are accessible permanently.

📊

Engagement History

Every project, every brief, every conversation — stored. When a returning client starts a new project, the context is already there. This is relationship memory, systematised.

🔒

Access & Compliance Ready

Role-based access, audit trails, and documented approvals support formal procurement environments. Built for the kind of clients who require sign-off accountability and version control.

How to Show It on the Site

The /platform Page Strategy

What to Show

  • ›A short video or animated walkthrough (no login required)
  • ›3–4 interface screenshots, lightly branded
  • ›Client quote specifically about the platform experience
  • ›The 6 benefit pillars (as named cards, not tech specs)
  • ›CTA: "Platform access is included with every engagement"

What Not to Show

  • ✕A login button on the main nav (keeps it exclusive)
  • ✕Any pricing or plan tiers for platform access
  • ✕Technical jargon or stack references
  • ✕Comparison to Trello, Asana, or other tools
  • ✕A "sign up free" flow — this is not self-serve
05 · Client Outreach

Reaching Existing Clients with Your Platform

1
Preparation

Segment Your Existing Client List

Divide into three groups before outreach: (A) Active clients mid-project, (B) Recently completed clients within 12 months, (C) Dormant clients not engaged in over a year. Each group receives a different message with a different tone and purpose.

2
Messaging

Frame It as an Upgrade, Not a Feature

Do not send "we built a new tool." Send "we've invested in the way we work together." The platform is a benefit to them — more clarity, less friction, better outcomes. Every word should make them feel like they're gaining something, not being asked to do something new.

3
Channel

Personal Email First — Not a Newsletter

Do not blast this via Mailchimp. For high-value clients, a personal email from a named senior contact carries ten times the weight. Reference their last project by name. Make it feel like a private introduction, not a broadcast. Reserve the newsletter format for a wider "studio news" send, months later.

4
Invitation

Invite to a Private Platform Walkthrough

Offer a 20-minute screen share: "I'd love to show you what we've built." This is not a sales call — it's a demonstration of investment in your relationship. It also creates a natural opportunity to re-engage on future work without asking for business directly.

5
Follow-Through

Onboard Their Historical Projects Into the Platform

Before the walkthrough call, pre-load their previous project assets and notes into their workspace. When they join the call and see their own work already organised there, the value is visceral. They didn't expect that. That's the moment the platform converts from a feature into a reason to stay.

06 · Voice & Positioning Language

What You Say vs. What Your Competitors Say

❌ Language That Undermines Premium Positioning

Tendering / Bids / Proposals
Signals a commodity process. You're not competing on price — you're being selected on capability and trust.
Unlimited revisions / Fast turnaround
DesignJoy's language. It signals volume and speed, not quality and craft. Premium clients aren't buying throughput.
We'd love to work with you / We're available
Sounds desperate. Premium agencies are selectively engaged, not available.
Affordable / Competitive pricing / Great value
Price-positioning language. Your clients aren't looking for affordable. They're looking for excellent.
Full-service agency / One-stop-shop
Vague and forgettable. Says nothing about what makes you different or who you're for.

✓ Language That Builds Authority & Attracts the Right Clients

Selected engagements / Commissioned work
Positions each project as curated and intentional. You choose your clients as much as they choose you.
Considered craft / Sustained quality
Signals deliberate, careful work — the antithesis of subscription-model throughput.
We work with organisations who…
Selective framing. Attracts the right clients by describing them. Makes prospects want to qualify themselves in.
Investment / Partnership / Engagement
Reframes cost as mutual commitment to shared outcomes. No pricing page needed when this language is consistent.
Strategic design studio / Built for ambition
Specific, elevated, memorable. Suggests you're a thinking partner — not a pair of hands. This is what Superside and DesignJoy can never claim credibly.
