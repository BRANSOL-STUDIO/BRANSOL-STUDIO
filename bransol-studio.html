Who we are

BRANSOL is a strategic design studio based in Ballito, KwaZulu-Natal. We work with institutions, listed companies, government entities, and ambitious private organisations on commissions that require more than decoration.

How we work

Selectively. We take on a limited number of engagements each year — not because of capacity, but because depth requires choice. Every client gets the studio's full attention, not a fraction of it.

What we believe

That design is not decoration. That strategy without execution is just a conversation. That the best work happens when client and studio operate as genuine partners — not vendor and buyer.

Philosophy

What we
actually
believe.

01
Design is a business argument, not a cosmetic one.
Every design decision has a business consequence. We treat aesthetics as a strategic instrument — not the goal, but the vehicle through which strategy becomes visible and legible to the people who matter.
02
The brief is half the work.
A poorly defined brief produces expensive revisions, not good work. We invest more time in understanding the problem than most studios spend on the entire project. When the brief is right, the design almost writes itself.
03
Selectivity is a form of respect.
We decline commissions that are not right — wrong fit, wrong timing, or where we genuinely believe another studio would serve the client better. This is not a negotiating tactic. It is how we stay honest.
04
Permanence over novelty.
Design that chases trends ages in years. Design built on enduring principles ages in decades. We are not interested in what is fashionable. We are interested in what will still be correct when you look back on it in ten years.
05
Craft is not optional.
In a world where speed tools can produce passable work instantly, the distinction between passable and excellent has never been more valuable. We spend time on the details that most people will never consciously notice — because those details are felt.
06
We tell clients when we disagree.
A studio that only agrees is not a partner — it is a production house. We are hired for our judgement. If we believe a direction is wrong, we say so directly, explain why, and propose an alternative. Clients pay for that, whether they know it or not.

What we stand for

Five principles.
Non-negotiable.

01
Honesty before harmony
We tell clients what we think, even when it is uncomfortable. We have lost commissions for this. We have kept clients for it. The clients we keep are the ones worth keeping.
02
Depth over volume
We are not trying to be the largest studio on the North Coast. We are trying to do the best work in our disciplines, for clients who understand the difference. Volume is the enemy of depth.
03
Accountability without excuses
If something goes wrong — and sometimes it does — we fix it, explain what happened, and apply what we learned. We do not blame briefs, budgets, or timelines. We own the output.
04
Senior involvement, always
Every commission is led by a senior practitioner with direct responsibility for the quality of the work. We do not have a layer of juniors working on client projects while seniors manage relationships.
05
Long relationships over quick wins
Our 94% client retention rate is the result of a simple commitment: we behave the same way at the end of a project as we did at the beginning. No drop in attention. No handover to a junior. No moving on.
Operating Model

How we
actually work.

Most agencies describe a process. We describe a set of commitments. These are the things that are true of every BRANSOL engagement — not aspirationally, but as a matter of how we are structured.

If any of these are not achievable for a given commission, we say so during the conversation before any work begins.

01
One brief. Documented. Agreed.
Every commission begins with a structured brief that is written, reviewed, and signed off by both parties before design work begins. Verbal instructions do not exist in BRANSOL's workflow. Ambiguity is resolved before execution, not during it.
Enforced by platform · No exceptions
02
Milestones, not months of silence.
Every project is broken into defined milestones with agreed deliverables and review dates. Clients see progress in the platform in real time — not at the end of a long black-box period when it is too late to redirect.
Synced to Asana · Visible on client platform
03
Feedback that lands.
Feedback is collected through structured channels — not email, not WhatsApp, not a voice note forwarded three times. Every comment is contextual, attributed, and resolved in writing. We close every feedback loop explicitly.
Structured · Documented · Resolved
04
Delivery that is actually complete.
A project is not done when the design is done. It is done when the files are organised, correctly named, correctly formatted, permanently archived, and the client knows exactly what they have and how to use it. We stay until that is true.

Who we work with

We are selective
on purpose.

We review every enquiry personally. We do not run a qualification funnel or a sales process. We have a conversation. If the commission is right — right fit, right timing, right client — we proceed. If it is not, we say so and we are honest about why.

We decline commissions when we believe another studio would serve the client better, when the timeline is incompatible with the quality required, or when the scope is outside our disciplines.

01
We respond to every enquiry
Even when the answer is no. Within two working days. Always personally — not a form response.
02
We do not negotiate on quality
We will negotiate scope. We will negotiate timeline. We will not produce work that we believe is below the standard that represents BRANSOL correctly — regardless of budget pressure.
03
We disclose when we collaborate
If we bring in a specialist collaborator — a photographer, fabricator, or developer — we tell you. You know who is working on your project at every stage.
04
We do not oversell the relationship
We do not tell every prospect that they are "exactly the kind of client we love to work with." If we say it, we mean it. Language is cheap. We let the work do the talking.
05
The studio is accessible
Clients have direct access to the senior person leading their project — not an account manager who passes messages. Direct is faster, more honest, and produces better work.
